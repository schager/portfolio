Our Titles

Once Upon a Broken Heart

Stephanie Garber

4.5☆

This was a very fun and magical read. This book doesn't take itself too seriously and has well-developed characters and world building.

Everything, Everything

Nicola Yoon

4.5☆

This book will take you on an emotional rollercoaster but I could not recommend it enough. It offers a very unique perspective on life and also tells a very sweet love story.

Find this book on Goodreads

The Sun Is Also a Star

Nicola Yoon

4☆

Although this book isn't a mystery, the story unravels and reveals itself to the reader through many characters perspectives. It's a really fun read and I find myself coming back to it often.

Find this book on Goodreads

Shatter Me

Tahereh Mafi

3.5☆

This is the first of a 6-book series. The book follows a girl with supernatural powers in a dystopian society. Although I prefer the later books in this series, this first book sets the tone well.

Find this book on Goodreads

Malibu Rising

Taylor Jenkins Reid

4.5☆

True to this author's reputation, this book is very complex with characters and storylines. I felt fully immersed in the beachy culture of Malibu in the 60s as well as the high-class culture of the 80s.

Find this book on Goodreads

Love and Other Words

Christina Lauren

5☆

This book is so sweet and highlights the experience of young love. All of the character relationships in the book feel so meaningful and they are what drive the book.

Find this book on Goodreads

The Cruel Prince

Holly Black

4☆

This book follows the story of a human girl growing up in a mystical land of fairies and mythological creatures. It's a great introduction to fantasy for readers who want to try out the genre.

Find this book on Goodreads

Powerless

Lauren Roberts

3.5☆

This book is another great introduction to the fantasy genre. The dystopian world-building is easy to follow and the book keeps you on the edge of your seat from beginning to end.
